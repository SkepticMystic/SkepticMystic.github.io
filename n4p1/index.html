The Role of Fake News since the 20th Century

Defining Fake News

Fake news is “false or misleading information presented as news and communicated via various mediums including:

  • spoken,
  • written,
  • printed, and
  • electronic communication.” [[hidgonAnatomyFakeNews2020 SN|(Hidgon, 2020)]]

South Africa

Many South African media sources have reported fake news as an increasingly used tool to:

  • increase distrust in the media,
  • discredit political opponents, and
  • divert attention from corruption [[danielsHowFakeNews2017 SN|(Daniels, 2017)]].

In 2016, the ANC was taken to court by Sihle Bolani for unpaid work she did for them [[mailguardianItFakeNews2017 SN|(Mail & Guardian, 2017)]].

Bolani stated that the ANC used her to launch and run a covert ==R50 million fake news and disinformation campaign== aimed at discrediting opposition parties [[encaANCSpentMillions2017 SN|(eNCA, 2017)]].

M ore than 6.6 million tweets [[hindmanDisinformationFakeNews2018 SN|(Hindman, 2017)]] and 100 incorrect articles [[allcottSocialMediaFake2017 SN|(Allcott & Gentzknow, 2017)]] linking to fake and conspiracy news publishers around the 2016 campaign .

Mechanism

Help the spread of fake news:

  • Political polarization,
  • Post-truth politics,
  • Confirmation bias, and
  • Social media algorithms [[tsangMotivatedFakeNews2020 SN|(Tsang, 2020)]].

A [[guessLessYouThink2019 SN|2019 study]] by Guess et al. found that the likelihood of sharing fake-news articles correlated most with age than it did education, sex, or political views.

  • 11% of users older than 65 shared fake news.
  • Just 3% of users ages 18 to 29 did the same

Tech-companies lack an incentive for solving the problem because they benefit from the proliferation of fake news. They make millions from collecting our data [[hidgonAnatomyFakeNews2020 SN|(Hidgon, 2020)]].

Rather than government regulation or industry censorship, Higdon argues for the introduction of ==critical news literacy education==.

Usage of the term

“It’s time to retire the tainted term ‘fake news’. Though the term hasn’t been around long, its meaning already is lost.”

By late 2018, U.S. journalists were asking for apologies and for product retirements from companies using the term [[mcmillanNewseumPullsFake2018 SN|(McMillan & Wootson Jr, 2018)]].

In October 2018, the British government decided that the term “fake news” will no longer be used in official documents because it is

“a poorly-defined and misleading term that conflates a variety of false information, from genuine error through to foreign interference in democratic processes.”

[[murphyGovernmentBansPhrase2018 SN|(Murphy, 2018)]]
